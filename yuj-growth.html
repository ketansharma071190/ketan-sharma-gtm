07 · Lead generation
yuj

Sell the diagnosis before the design. That is how you earn a CPO's reply.

Who we target
  • CPOs & VPs Product at US cyber, fintech, healthtech, AI-SaaS.
  • Trigger signals: funding, a redesign or design-system hire, an AI launch, 1-star app reviews, an enterprise up-market push.
  • Tier 1 = UX friction visibly slows revenue, adoption, or the sales demo.
Sequence logic
  • Lead with a named friction hypothesis, not a brochure.
  • Quantify the business cost of that friction.
  • Offer a free Design Friction Index teardown of one workflow. Low risk, high signal.
  • Multi-thread: economic buyer, design exec, product validator.
Success metric
  • North star: qualified DFI teardowns booked / month (the real leading indicator).
  • Then: teardown → paid diagnostic conversion.
  • Then: diagnostic → retainer conversion + pipeline ARR created.

The teardown is the whole wedge. It is useful on its own, it proves our thinking before they spend a rupee, and it makes the first meeting about their product, not our portfolio.

08 · The outreach
yuj

Three emails to a CPO. Two LinkedIn notes. Each earns the next reply.

Email 1 · the friction hypothesis

Subject: your admin setup vs your activation curve

"Your platform is powerful, but the setup path looks built for experts. If new accounts need a human to reach value, design has quietly become a revenue problem, not a polish one. Seeing that pattern in your space a lot right now."

Email 2 · the business cost

"Usually the cost hides in support tickets, slow onboarding, weaker demos, and PMs shipping around the same confusion twice. We turn that into a friction map with a number attached. One chart, your product."

Email 3 · the low-risk ask

"Worth a 30-minute Design Friction Index teardown on one workflow? If nothing material surfaces, you lose half an hour. If it does, you leave with a funded redesign case."

LinkedIn · cold

"Noticed you're pushing deeper into enterprise. That's usually where the product gets more powerful and onboarding quietly starts dragging adoption. I'm with YUJ; we turn that friction into a roadmap executives fund. Worth comparing notes on one workflow?"

LinkedIn · referral

"Trying to reach whoever owns product adoption / design maturity at [Company]. Not a 'do you need UX' question, more whether one complex workflow is slowing sales or onboarding. Could you point me to the right product or design leader?"

Copy principle

Sound like a useful operator who already understands their product, never a vendor reading from a deck. Specific hypothesis first, business impact second, the ask last.
