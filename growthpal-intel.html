THE CATEGORY SHIFT 01

Owning the data is no longer the moat.

Three things are happening at once, and they all point the same direction: value is leaving the database and moving to the deal.

01 · CONSOLIDATING

The data layer is being rolled up.

Datasite acquired Grata (~$200M+, announced Jun 2025), then bought rival Sourcescrub (Aug 2025) and is merging it in. The independent sourcing-data players are becoming one unit inside a deal-execution incumbent.

Signal: raw data and search are commoditizing fast enough that the prize is now consolidation, not differentiation.

02 · COOLING

The research-first incumbent is maturing.

PitchBook runs ~80% recurring on breadth, but it sells research, not outcomes. Its own jab, "if it's in PitchBook, it's too late," is the tell: by the time a target is in the database, the edge is gone.

Signal: the buyer increasingly pays for what is not in the database yet.

03 · MIGRATING

Value moves to the outcome.

Every dollar is shifting from "here is the data" to "here is the deal." Sourcing fused with execution, sold as a closed result. That is the layer the roll-ups are reaching toward, and the layer we already stand on.

Signal: the winner sells the LOI, not the list.

THE TEARDOWNS 04

Grata

Status
Acquired by Datasite (~$200M+, Jun 2025). Datasite then bought Sourcescrub and is merging it into Grata. Founded 2016 NY, ~$35M raised.

PE, IBs, corp dev, family offices. Pure SaaS in 3 persona tiers (Growth → Scale → Alpha), all demo-gated.

Grata sells data and search; the buyer still does the work. We reason from thesis to target and run it to LOI. We sell the outcome, and the roll-up proves the value is migrating exactly where we already sit.

THE PROOF IT COMPOUNDS

Small, high-frequency M&A is the winning strategy.

McKinsey's programmatic finding: a minimum of two small or mid deals a year beats peers by 20%+ over a decade, at lower risk. Constellation Software ran 1,000+ vertical-software acquisitions, mostly under $5M, into ~22% revenue CAGR. The long tail of small deals is where compounding lives, and it is exactly the band we serve.

THE WEDGE

Value is concentrating up; the long tail is left to us.

Deal value is up ~36% while deal count is up only ~5%, so value piles into mega-deals and bankers chase those. The sub-$100M, founder-owned, off-market companies are underserved by bankers. Find them before the bankers do and you lift the buyer's employee-sourced rate and cross the programmatic-outperformance line at once.

Sources: Bain 2025 M&A Report; McKinsey programmatic M&A; Deloitte corporate development survey; company filings (Accenture, Constellation Software, Cisco).

The whole game is turning a spiky success fee into a base that re-rates.

Recurring re-rates like SaaS and climbs toward the 20x-and-up that best-in-class commands; a success fee trades like a service, around 1.5x. NRR above 120% adds a further multiple premium. The gap between the rungs is the prize.

The proven shape, and the levers to get there.

Intapp/DealCloud posts 124% cloud NRR on a committed base plus module expansion (834 clients over $100k ARR). Hybrid models out-earn pure ones: hybrid firms run ~38% higher NRR and growth. Here is how the success fee becomes recurring without cannibalizing itself.

A

Creditable retainer

A monthly retainer creditable against the success fee at close. Spiky becomes steady; ~72% of advisors already net the retainer against the fee.

B

Sector-exclusive mandates + tail

12–18 month multi-year mandates with a 6–12 month tail clause. Locked recurring plus post-term coverage on deals we sourced.

C

Committed minimums

A minimum per mandate so the base is contracted, not hoped for. The floor is real ARR before any deal closes.

D

Prepaid origination credits

A committed wallet burned against intros and outcomes, the Inven credit lever pointed at deals instead of rows of data.

E

Price the base on coverage

Charge the subscription on coverage (mandates × geographies), the success fee on the outcome (deals closed). The two never cannibalize.

F

Report it clean

Committed ARR vs Usage ARR, with NRR reported separately. The board sees a forecastable base and a variable upside, not one blurred line.

STICKINESS THAT RECURS ACROSS WINNERS

Become the system of record, then expansion is free.

Auto-capture kills the adoption problem (Affinity hit 96% adoption at Munich Re Ventures). Two-way CRM sync into DealCloud/Affinity/Salesforce makes us the corp-dev system of record. Always-on alerts turn episodic research into daily use. A predictive, relationship-intelligence data loop deepens with every deal worked.

THE PILOT IS THE SALE

Land on proof, expand on workflow.

A 30-day time-boxed bake-off on the customer's real data, the "show me a net-new deal I haven't seen" test, adoption measured at 30/60/90. Land at $20–60k, expand to six figures through seats, modules and coverage, exactly the DealCloud expansion shape, on a base we already align to the outcome.

Sources: Intapp investor filings (Dec 2025); Bessemer / OpenView / ICONIQ usage-based NRR benchmarks; Affinity case study (Munich Re Ventures); M&A advisory retainer-structure norms.
