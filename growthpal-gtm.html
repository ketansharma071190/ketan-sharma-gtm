From founder-led selling to a repeatable global engine.

An outside read on the US and EU go-to-market for GrowthPal, and the structure to scale it. A starting point for a conversation with Maneesh, not a finished plan. From someone who builds these engines.

Ketan Sharma  ·  Enterprise GTM  ·  Pune  ·  ketansharma071190@gmail.com
01
Where the edge actually is

Your edge is not a better database. It is the outcome.

  • Grata, Sourcescrub, Cyndx and PitchBook sell data and search. The buyer still does the work.
  • GrowthPal does the thesis-to-target reasoning, then puts a human banking team on validation and outreach to LOI.
  • You win the sub-70M off-market band that bankers ignore and the data tools under-serve.
  • So the go-to-market sells the outcome and the discretion, not a feature list. That is how financial-services buyers actually buy.
02
Who you sell to

Four buyers, one shape: senior, consultative, trust-first.

  • Corp-dev and strategy teams, the Razorpay and PhonePe and Salesforce sweet spot, buy on time-to-target and proprietary flow.
  • PE and PE-backed platforms doing roll-ups buy proprietary flow to dodge auctions and banker fees, and benchmark hard against their stack.
  • Investment banks and boutiques buy leverage to win and service the sub-70M mandates they cannot economically staff.
  • Across all four the economic buyer is a CFO, a corp-dev head, a PE partner. Exactly the financial-services C-suite I sell to.
03
The honest fit

The buyer is my home turf. The category is my ramp.

  • Home turf: complex AI and data products into financial-services and C-suite buyers. Simplifai 0 to 1.6M ARR, anchors closed personally at Kotak, HDFC MF, Bajaj, Upstox.
  • Home turf: founder-led to repeatable. ZenAdmin's commercial org built to run without me.
  • Home turf: international enterprise GTM, US and EU at ZenAdmin.
  • The ramp: M&A and deal-intelligence as a category. I climb it fast because I rebuild the motion from first principles every time.
04
The real problem to solve

The core GTM problem is your hybrid model, not finding a closer.

  • Revenue is subscription plus success-fee-on-close. Lumpy, and the opposite of the predictable engine investors want.
  • You cannot just hand AEs a quota when half the value is advisory judgment you only partly control.
  • So the build is three things at once: codify the intake, guarantee sourcing quality, separate the repeatable motion from the bespoke.
  • The hardest job here is engineering predictability on top of a contingent revenue line.
05
The motion

Make committed subscription the forecastable base. Success fees are the upside.

  • Push committed-mandate subscription, priced by mandates and geographies, as the forecastable revenue spine.
  • Treat success fees as upside, never as the number the team forecasts against.
  • Land-and-expand is the engine. The client who closed 7 acquisitions in 18 months is the template for retention through more mandates and geographies.
  • Predictability comes from committed mandates and a sourcing-quality SLA the client trusts, not from hoping deals close.
06
The structure

This is a services-blended engine, not a pure SaaS team.

  • Codify thesis-to-mandate intake so it lives in a playbook, not only in Maneesh's head.
  • Build BDRs and AEs for the land motion, orchestrated with the in-house banking and analyst team that validates and runs outreach.
  • The sourcing-quality SLA is the trust contract. A signed mandate that does not produce trusted targets churns and kills the success fee.
  • Land, activate, prove sourcing quality. GTM and delivery are fused, or the model breaks.
07
Risks, named not hidden

The two real GTM risks, on the table.

  • Trust in a discretion-first, relationship-driven world: lead with proof of proprietary off-market flow and references, the 7-in-18-months story, not features.
  • Adoption and behavior change: the binding constraint in AI-for-M&A is people, not technology. Onboarding and activation are part of the sale.
  • Coexisting with installed Grata, Sourcescrub and Affinity stacks at PE buyers: slot in as origination, do not pick a database fight.
  • I would rather walk in naming these than pretend the motion is frictionless.
08

  • I earn trust with the exact financial-services C-suite buyer GrowthPal sells to, on complex AI products, from zero.
  • I run services-blended motions, not just clean SaaS. Dineout on the ground and my own profitable brand prove it.
  • I think like an owner, which is why I build durable engines that outlast me. I want to build this one deeply.
09
The first six months

First 180 days: 30 / 90 / 180.

First 30 days
Ride every live deal with Maneesh. Map the real motion. Write down the thesis-to-mandate intake. Separate committed from contingent in the forecast.
By 90 days
Ship the playbook and operating cadence. Define the sourcing-quality SLA. Make the first repeatable AE hires. Close one marquee US or EU deal myself.
By 180 days
A forecastable subscription base. A US and EU pipeline that does not route through the founder. A land-and-expand motion live. A team running the engine.

Throughout, I take selling off Maneesh's back deal by deal, never all at once, to protect the trust that closes today.
