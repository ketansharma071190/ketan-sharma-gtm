Your edge is accuracy. The go-to-market should be built around it.

An outside read on the commercial engine for Map My Crop, and the structure to scale it. From someone who builds them.

Ketan Sharma  ·  Enterprise GTM  ·  Pune  ·  ketansharma071190@gmail.com
01
Start with what makes you different

Your moat is accuracy. The closest-to-true yield in the field. That is a commercial weapon, not just a data point.

Most agri data is a commodity. A prediction a buyer can trust enough to act on is not. The whole go-to-market should be built to sell that one difference, because it is the thing no competitor can copy quickly.

02
The honest read on the go-to-market

Right now the best data in the field is sold to whoever will buy it.

  • You have proven the product works, roughly $1M, mostly won farmer by farmer.
  • But selling it one farmer at a time treats a precision instrument like a commodity.
  • An edge like accuracy is worth the most to whoever takes financial risk on being right.
  • The work is to point the go-to-market at the buyers who pay for that, and to build the structure that makes it repeatable.
03
The shift

Sell the outcome your accuracy creates, not the map.

  • Nobody writes a cheque for NDVI. They pay for fewer surprises, a lower loss ratio, a better planting call.
  • The more financial weight a buyer puts on being right, the more your accuracy is worth to them.
  • That reframes the entire motion: from acreage and features to the decision your number improves.
  • Accuracy is not a spec on a slide. It is pricing power, if the go-to-market is built to capture it.
04
Where the edge is worth the most

The same prediction is worth a different order of magnitude depending on who acts on it.

A yield call is worth a few thousand rupees to a farmer. To an insurer, a lender, or an input major planning a season on it, the same call is worth many times more, because they are betting real money on it being right.

05
The structure

The commercial function, run as a machine you forecast against, not in the founders’ heads.

  • Stages: ICP, Qualify, Co-create the pilot, Land, Expand.
  • Qualify bar: does the buyer carry financial risk your accuracy reduces? If not, disqualify fast.
  • It lives in a CRM, with one number reviewed every week.
  • A clean handoff to delivery, and a post-sale signal, so a landed account expands instead of churning.
06
The motion

One focused, named-account motion beats selling to everyone everywhere.

  • Pick the segment where accuracy commands the most, prove the number on a real pilot, then expand from strength.
  • A value-led sequence built on the decision your data improves, not a feature demo.
  • Land with a tightly scoped pilot, grow it into a platform relationship.
  • Build the deck, the demo-to-deal motion, and a CRM the team actually runs on.
07
The biggest version of the edge

The institution that underwrites on your number is the lane you are already heading to.

  • Insurers and lenders are where accuracy turns straight into their P&L, the BFSI direction you are planning for after the raise.
  • The motion and structure I build now are what make you ready to win that lane the day you go for it, not starting from zero.
  • The regulatory tailwind is already there: PMFBY and YES-TECH are turning satellite yield into a budget line for insurers.
  • SatSure proved the lane is real and bankable. Your accuracy edge is the reason to believe you can take it.
