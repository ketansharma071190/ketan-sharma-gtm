Founder-Led Sales Diagnostic

If you are still closing every serious enterprise deal yourself, something is broken.

This is a focused diagnostic for B2B SaaS and AI founders with early traction, real pipeline, and a sales motion that still depends too much on founder judgment. The goal is not a strategy deck. The goal is to find what is breaking, what to stop doing, and what to rebuild first.

When to use this

You do not need more sales activity. You need to know where the motion is failing.

The founder is still needed in most serious deals.

First reps are working hard but not reproducing founder-level judgment.

Enterprise pipeline exists, but qualification and next steps are inconsistent.

Outbound creates meetings, but opportunities do not survive discovery.

The company is hiring Head of Sales, but the real issue may be ICP, process, or handoff.

CRM says one thing, founder instinct says another, and nobody trusts the forecast.

What gets inspected

The diagnostic looks at the actual operating system, not the sales story.

01

ICP quality

Won, lost, stalled, and founder-rescued deals are reviewed to expose which accounts are real and which only look good in a spreadsheet.

02

Qualification

I compare how the founder qualifies against how the team qualifies: pain, urgency, authority, buying committee, timing, budget, and disqualification.

03

Lost-deal teardown

We identify whether deals are being lost because of wrong buyer, weak proof, pricing fear, stakeholder gaps, product risk, procurement, or sales process.

04

Founder handoff

The founder's sales instinct is mapped into discovery, objection handling, proof, pricing logic, stakeholder control, and close planning.

05

First-rep gap

I assess whether the current or next sales hire is the right profile for this motion, and what coaching or operating cadence they need.

06

30-day rebuild plan

The output is a practical plan: what to fix first, what to stop, what to test, what to automate, and what founder involvement should change.

How it runs

A short sprint to find the truth and turn it into operating decisions.

Day 1-2

Founder and context intake

Stage, motion, ICP, pricing, current team, top deals, stuck deals, lost deals, and what the founder already suspects is broken.

Day 3-5

Pipeline and call inspection

CRM, recent calls, outbound, proposals, handoffs, follow-ups, deal stages, and proof used in live enterprise conversations.

Day 6-8

Pattern diagnosis

Where the motion is leaking: ICP, qualification, narrative, stakeholder control, pricing, proof, handoff, hiring, or operating cadence.

Day 9-10

Operating readout

A direct readout with the first 30-day plan, what to stop doing, and whether the next step should be advisory, fractional, interim, or full-time.
