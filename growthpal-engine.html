Your edge
04 / 11
What I learned from Inven, Grata, PitchBook and Capital IQ

They sell the database. You sell the outcome. That is where value is migrating.

  • The data layer is commoditizing and consolidating. Datasite rolled up Grata and Sourcescrub, the two biggest sourcing datasets, into one owner. Owning a list is no longer the moat.
  • Even the leader is cooling, in your buyer. PitchBook's net revenue retention fell from 127% to about 103% and seats declined in 2025, with the churn concentrated in the corporate segment, the exact buyer you are chasing.
  • The winners lock in on workflow, not data. Capital IQ's Excel plug-in and academic certification, Grata and Affinity's two-way CRM sync. The product becomes the system of record, then it cannot be ripped out.
  • GrowthPal already does the part they do not. Thesis to precision target to a human banking team to LOI. You sell a closed outcome, not a search box. Build the recurring engine on top of that and you leapfrog the category.

The fix
06 / 11
Make the recurring line the spine, the success fee the kicker

The committed-mandate subscription is the forecastable base. Everything else is upside.

  • Price the base on coverage, not closes. Subscription on mandates times geographies, so the recurring line never competes with the outcome. Your public tiers, $129 / $299 / $1,000 per user, are the seed of this.
  • Make the success fee creditable. A draw-down retainer credited against the fee at close turns spiky into steady, the move roughly 70% of M&A advisors already use. Add prepaid origination credits for committed-upfront, burn-on-use revenue.
  • Lock duration in. Multi-year, sector-exclusive mandates with tail clauses convert one engagement into always-on coverage and post-term economics.
  • Report it like the winners. Committed ARR and Usage ARR shown separately, with NRR. DealCloud runs this exact shape, committed base plus expansion, at 124% net revenue retention.
Stickiness
07 / 11
Stickiness is the NRR, and the NRR is the valuation

Make the platform un-rippable. Become the corp-dev system of record.

  • Two-way CRM sync into DealCloud, Affinity and Salesforce. The move every winner made. Once your sourcing feeds their pipeline daily, leaving means stale data across the whole funnel.
  • Always-on, not episodic. Saved theses, live market mapping and intent alerts convert a one-time search into a daily-use subscription, the reason to renew.
  • Land and expand by seats and modules. Land a thesis, grow into more mandates, geographies and the analyst seats around the champion. Auto-capture kills the adoption problem, the lever that took Affinity to 96% firm-wide adoption.
  • A data moat that compounds. Off-market, proprietary and predictive signal that gets sharper with every deal the human team validates. That loop is yours, not a reseller's.
The motion
08 / 11
Focused where it pays: US 85%, Europe 15%

A named-account motion into serial tech and IT acquirers and PE.

  • Multi-thread the three buyers. The champion is the VP of corporate development, the economic buyer is the CFO or CSO, the power users are the analysts. Engage all three in parallel.
  • The pilot is the sale. A 30-day, time-boxed bake-off on their real data, with one test: show me an off-market target I have not seen. Time-to-value inside 30 days is the published bar.
  • De-risk the slow part early. A pre-filled security and procurement pack on day one removes the longest stall in enterprise cycles.
  • Two speeds, one playbook. PE lands fast on urgency, corporates land slower but stickier and bigger. Channel through data integrations and M&A advisors who already own the acquirer relationships. India stays deprioritized, correctly, on ACV.
The build
09 / 11
Bang on, not rushed, your words

Hire precision into a proven motion, not headcount into a hope.

  • Founder-led first. Maneesh stays on the marquee deals while we codify exactly how he qualifies, sources and closes, until the motion repeats on its own.
  • Prove, then hire. Roughly 10 to 15 reference closes through a documented process before the first dedicated AE, so every hire drops into a machine, not a blank page.
  • Right shape of seller. A trust-first, judgment-led seller for a high-trust buyer, not a volume rep. AI works the broad universe, humans work the named accounts.
This is the opposite of "let's hire 10 people." The discipline is to be precise about the one motion that works before scaling the team around it, which is exactly the bar you set.
