Our edge
04 / 10
Where the value is migrating

They sell the database. We sell the outcome.

  • The data layer is consolidating into a commodity. Datasite now owns both Grata and Sourcescrub. A clean list is table stakes, not a moat.
  • Even the leader is cooling, in our buyer. PitchBook's net revenue retention slipped from 127% to about 103% and seats fell in 2025, with the churn in the corporate segment, the exact buyer we go after.
  • The winners lock in on workflow, not data. The Excel plug-in, two-way CRM sync. The product becomes the system of record, then it cannot be ripped out. That is the playbook we run.
  • We already do the part they cannot. Thesis to precision target to a human banking team to LOI. We sell a closed outcome. Put the recurring engine on top of that and we leapfrog the category.

The fix
06 / 10
Make the recurring line the spine, the success fee the kicker

We make the committed-mandate subscription the forecastable base.

  • We price the base on coverage, not closes. Subscription on mandates times geographies, so the recurring line never competes with the outcome. Our public tiers, $129 / $299 / $1,000 per user, are the seed of this.
  • We make the success fee creditable. A draw-down retainer credited against the fee at close turns spiky into steady, what most M&A advisors already do. Prepaid origination credits add committed-upfront revenue.
  • We lock duration in. Multi-year, sector-exclusive mandates with tail clauses convert one engagement into always-on coverage and post-term economics.
  • We report it like the winners. Committed ARR and Usage ARR shown separately, with NRR. DealCloud runs this exact shape, committed base plus expansion, at 124% net revenue retention.
Stickiness
07 / 10
Stickiness is the NRR, and the NRR is the valuation

We make the platform un-rippable: the corp-dev system of record.

  • Two-way CRM sync into DealCloud, Affinity and Salesforce. Once our sourcing feeds their pipeline daily, leaving means stale data across the whole funnel.
  • Always-on, not episodic. Saved theses, live market mapping and intent alerts turn a one-time search into a daily-use subscription, the reason to renew.
  • Land and expand by seats and modules. More mandates, more geographies, the analyst seats around the champion. Auto-capture kills the adoption problem, the lever that took Affinity to 96% firm-wide adoption.
  • A data moat that compounds. Off-market, proprietary, predictive signal that sharpens with every deal the human team validates. That loop is ours, not a reseller's.
The motion
08 / 10
Focused where it pays: US 85%, Europe 15%

A named-account motion into serial tech and IT acquirers and PE.

  • Multi-thread the three buyers. Champion the VP of corporate development, sell the CFO or CSO, win the analysts who use it daily.
  • The pilot is the sale. A 30-day bake-off on their real data, one test: surface an off-market target they have not seen. Value inside 30 days is the bar.
  • We take a position on geography. Build the US and EU motion from the ground, not run from India. The India seat keeps the local edge, the new muscle goes where the deals and the dollars are.
  • Channel for leverage. A data feed inside DealCloud and Affinity, co-sold with the M&A advisors who already own the acquirer relationships. De-risk procurement on day one.
The build
09 / 10
Precision, not headcount

We hire into a proven motion, never into a hope.

  • Founder-led first. Maneesh stays on the marquee deals while we codify exactly how he qualifies, sources and closes, until the motion repeats without him.
  • Prove, then hire. Ten to fifteen reference closes through a documented process before the first dedicated AE, so every hire drops into a machine, not a blank page.
  • The right shape of seller. Trust-first and judgment-led, for a high-trust buyer. AI works the broad universe, humans work the named accounts.
